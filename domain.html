Domain Overview

Literature survey, research gaps, problem statement, objectives, methodology, and technologies used across all four research components.

Background

Literature Survey

1.1 Last-Mile Delivery — The Operational Context

Last-mile delivery refers to the final stage in a parcel's journey from a depot or sortation centre to the delivery address. This stage is characterised by high variability in spatial, temporal, and social context. The combination of these factors creates a 'trust boundary in an uncontrolled environment' — a point at which the verification burden is high and the available evidence is typically weak.

Existing verification mechanisms are grouped into three categories: knowledge-based (OTPs, PINs), possession-based (physical ID, QR codes), and biometric (fingerprint, face, voice). Biometric mechanisms uniquely bind verification to the physical person and, when combined with liveness detection, are resistant to artefact-based spoofing.

Global B2C e-commerce revenues projected to exceed USD 6.3 trillion by 2025. Last-mile accounts for 41–53% of total supply chain expenditure. [ITC, 2022]

1.2 Sinhala Natural Language Processing and Voice AI

Sinhala is the native language of approximately 74% of Sri Lanka's population. It is characterised by a rich morphological system, syllabic script, and prosodic features distinct from European languages. The resource scarcity of Sinhala in NLP training data has historically constrained the development and accuracy of automated Sinhala speech processing systems.

Recent advances: OpenAI Whisper (680,000 hours multilingual) demonstrates meaningful Sinhala ASR without language-specific fine-tuning. Google's multilingual TTS and Gemini's Native Speech API provide Sinhala synthesis with meaningfully improved naturalness over prior rule-based approaches.

1.3 Voice Biometrics and Anti-Spoofing

Speaker verification confirms whether a speech segment was produced by a claimed speaker. The dominant modern approach is embedding-based: a deep neural network maps variable-length speech segments to fixed-dimensional representations, and cosine similarity serves as a verification score. ECAPA-TDNN and WavLM-based models represent the current state of the art, achieving sub-1% EER on standard benchmarks.

The emergence of high-fidelity neural TTS and voice conversion systems has introduced the threat of AI-generated synthetic voice. Spectral and prosodic analysis approaches complement dedicated neural anti-spoofing detectors, providing defense-in-depth against systems specifically tuned to evade a single detection model.

1.4 Multi-Stop Route Optimization and Weather-Aware Planning

Multi-stop route optimization is a variant of the Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP) — NP-hard in its general form. For small-scale instances typical of last-mile delivery (2–10 stops), heuristic approaches including nearest-neighbour construction and 2-opt improvement produce high-quality routes within acceptable latency bounds.

Weather-aware routing has received growing research attention. Rain, storm, and reduced-visibility conditions correlate with higher accident rates, increased delivery attempt failure rates, and elevated fuel consumption. Integration of real-time weather data from Open-Meteo into route planning tools represents a practical and underexplored approach to proactive risk management.

1.5 Face Recognition and Multimodal Biometric Fusion

Face recognition using deep neural networks has progressed to near-superhuman performance. ArcFace, which applies additive angular margin loss in normalised embedding space, has become the dominant paradigm for face verification. In video-based face verification, quality-aware frame aggregation strategies are essential for robust performance across diverse capture conditions.

Multimodal biometric fusion, combining evidence from two or more independent modalities, offers principled mitigation for limitations of unimodal approaches. Score-level fusion is the most widely adopted approach due to its modularity and amenability to principled calibration.

Problem Statement

Research Problem

Courier operations in Sri Lanka face converging failures across customer accessibility, delivery security, route efficiency, and operational risk awareness that individually-deployed tools cannot adequately resolve.

The absence of a Sinhala-language voice AI means that the majority language community cannot self-serve inquiry resolution. The reliance on knowledge-based and possession-based identity verification at parcel handover leaves deliveries vulnerable to fraud and impersonation.

The unavailability of integrated multi-stop route planning with weather and priority awareness results in operationally suboptimal route sequences and unmitigated weather risk exposure. The lack of biometric identity verification means that parcel handover cannot be reliably bound to the correct recipient's physical identity.

🌐

Accessibility

74% of Sri Lanka's population communicates in Sinhala — a language systematically underrepresented in automated customer support systems.

🛡️

Security

OTP and possession-based verification at parcel handover are fundamentally transferable and vulnerable to social engineering and AI-generated synthetic voice attacks.

📍

Efficiency

No existing tool simultaneously optimises multi-stop routes for distance efficiency, respects delivery urgency hierarchies, and surfaces weather hazards to couriers before dispatch.

Approach

Research Methodology

Each component follows a Design Science Research (DSR) paradigm in which a technological artefact is designed, implemented, and empirically evaluated within a realistic operational context. All four components share a common backend stack (FastAPI, MySQL, React) with a unified API gateway.

🎙️ C1 — Sinhala Voice Agent

  • Iterative three-stage architecture development
  • Stage 1: Whisper + Gemini + Azure TTS (6.8s, MOS 2.8)
  • Stage 2: Whisper + Gemini + gTTS (6.1s, MOS 3.4)
  • Stage 3: Gemini 2.0 Flash Native Speech (2.1s, MOS 4.7)
  • WebSocket streaming; MySQL function calling
  • MOS evaluation with 15 Sinhala-speaking participants

🔐 C2 — Voice Authentication

  • 7-step analyze_voice_sample pipeline
  • ElevenLabs voice isolation (Step 3)
  • Undetectable.AI external detection (Step 4)
  • Spectral + prosodic local analysis (Steps 5–6)
  • Confidence-weighted ensemble fusion (Step 7)
  • Three-tier risk decisions + step-up flag

🗺️ C3 — Route Optimizer

  • Google Maps DirectionsService (optimizeWaypoints)
  • Haversine distance for leg classification
  • Open-Meteo API — no API key, WMO severity codes
  • Sample points: stops + midpoints + quarter-points
  • Client-side priority pre-sorting (urgency order 0–3)
  • Real-time traffic via drivingOptions

👁️ C4 — Multimodal Face Engine

  • ArcFace (ResNet-100, MS1MV2) face pipeline
  • WavLM-Base-Plus-SV + ECAPA-TDNN ensemble
  • Quality-gated weighted score fusion
  • Tokenised one-time link + WebRTC live call workflows
  • Dataset: 48 participants, 4 scenarios
  • DSR phases: requirements → construction → calibration → evaluation
