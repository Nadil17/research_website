Research Components

Four Complementary AI Systems

Each component addresses a distinct but interrelated challenge in modern last-mile courier operations within a shared platform infrastructure.

Component 01
🎙️

Automated Sinhala AI Voice Agent

A voice-based customer inquiry system for Sinhala-speaking courier customers. Evolved through three architectural stages to achieve sub-3-second end-to-end latency and 4.7/5 MOS naturalness using Gemini 2.0 Flash Native Speech API.

Voice AI · NLP · ASR/TTS
Component 02
🔐

Voice Authentication System

A seven-step AI biometric pipeline for recipient identity confirmation at parcel handover. Combines ElevenLabs voice isolation, Undetectable.AI neural detection, and spectral/prosodic analysis through confidence-weighted ensemble fusion.

Anti-Spoofing · Biometrics · Ensemble
Component 03
🗺️

Route Optimizer

A React-based multi-stop route optimization app integrating Google Maps DirectionsService, Open-Meteo live weather sampling, and a four-level priority mode. Achieves 23.4% average distance savings and 100% priority sequencing correctness.

TSP · Weather-Aware · React
Component 04
👁️

Multimodal AI Face Engine

A production-grade biometric verification engine fusing ArcFace face verification and a WavLM/ECAPA-TDNN voice ensemble through quality-gated weighted score fusion. Supports tokenised one-time links and live WebRTC call verification.

ArcFace · WavLM · Multimodal Fusion

Project Overview

Last-Mile Delivery Reimagined

The Smart Courier Intelligence Platform is a group research project developed at the Sri Lanka Institute of Information Technology (SLIIT) that addresses four distinct but deeply interrelated operational challenges in modern courier systems through AI-driven technological solutions.

The last-mile delivery segment accounts for an estimated 41–53% of total supply chain expenditure, and remains the segment with the greatest surface area for security vulnerabilities, customer service failures, and operational inefficiency. This platform directly targets all four dimensions simultaneously.

The motivation arises from a convergence of gaps: identity verification remains poorly served by OTP-based mechanisms; route planning lacks weather and priority awareness; Sinhala-speaking customers are excluded from automated support; and synthetic voice threats challenge existing authentication systems.
